# THM-075: H=21 Permanent Gap Conjecture

**Status:** CONJECTURED (exhaustive through n=7, sampling through n=9)
**Discovered by:** kind-pasteur-2026-03-07-S31

## Statement

**Conjecture:** For any tournament T on any number of vertices n, H(T) != 21.

Equivalently: there is no tournament whose conflict graph Omega(T) has independence
polynomial I(Omega(T), x) satisfying I(Omega(T), 2) = 21.

## Evidence

### Sampling verification

| n | Samples | H=21 found? |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 8 | 100,000 | NO |

### Transient vs permanent gaps

The n=6 gaps at H=35 and H=39 are **filled** at n=7. The n=7 gaps at H=63, 107, 119
are **filled** at n=8 (sampling). Only H=7 and H=21 persist across all tested n.

## Structural Analysis

### Multiplicative monoid from non-overlapping flips

For the transitive tournament with a single edge flip of "range" k (flipping edge (i, i+k)):

  H = 1 + 2^{k-1}  (for k >= 2)

For two non-overlapping flips (disjoint vertex ranges) of ranges k1, k2 >= 2:

  H = (1 + 2^{k1-1}) * (1 + 2^{k2-1})

This multiplicativity follows from I(G1 + G2, x) = I(G1, x) * I(G2, x) for
disjoint union of Omega components.

The achievable H values from non-overlapping flips form the **multiplicative monoid**
generated by S = {1 + 2^k : k >= 1} = {3, 5, 9, 17, 33, 65, ...}.

Neither 7 nor 21 belong to this monoid:
- 7 is prime and 7 != 1 + 2^k for any k.
- 21 = 3 * 7, and 7 is not in S.

### Why the monoid structure is insufficient

The monoid explains the difficulty but not the impossibility. Overlapping flips and
general tournament structures produce H values outside the monoid (e.g., H=11, 13, 63).
Yet 7 and 21 remain unachievable even with these additional constructions.

### H=21 decomposition analysis

For H = 1 + 2*alpha_1 + 4*alpha_2 + ... = 21, need alpha_1 + 2*alpha_2 + ... = 10.

Decompositions:
- (alpha_1=10, alpha_2=0): 10 directed cycles, all pairwise conflicting.
- (alpha_1=8, alpha_2=1): 8 cycles with exactly 1 vertex-disjoint pair.
- (alpha_1=6, alpha_2=2): 6 cycles with exactly 2 disjoint pairs.
- (alpha_1=4, alpha_2=3): 4 cycles with exactly 3 disjoint pairs.
- (alpha_1=2, alpha_2=4): IMPOSSIBLE (C(2,2)=1 < 4).
- (alpha_1=0, alpha_2=5): IMPOSSIBLE (no cycles => no pairs).

At n=7 exhaustive, NONE of the valid decompositions are achieved by any tournament.

## Connection to THM-029 (H=7 impossibility)

THM-029 proves H=7 is impossible via: alpha_1=3, alpha_2=0 forces common vertex
forces 5-cycle forces alpha_1 >= 4.

A similar approach for H=21 would need to show that EACH of the 4 valid decompositions
(10,0), (8,1), (6,2), (4,3) leads to a contradiction. This is substantially harder
because the decomposition space is larger.
