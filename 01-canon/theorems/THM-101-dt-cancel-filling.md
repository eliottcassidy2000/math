# THM-101: DT+Cancellation Filling Theorem

**Status:** COMPUTATIONALLY VERIFIED (exhaustive n≤6)
**Proposed by:** opus-2026-03-08-S43

## Statement

**Theorem (Computational):** For any tournament T on n vertices, the 2-cycle space
Z₂ = ker(∂₂|Ω₂) is contained in the image of ∂₃ restricted to the subspace
of Ω₃ generated by:

1. **DT 4-paths**: (a,b,c,d) ∈ A₃ with a→c AND b→d (doubly-transitive)
2. **Cancellation pairs**: p₁ - p₂ where p₁, p₂ ∈ A₃ share the same bad 2-face

Consequently, im(∂₃) ⊇ Z₂, proving β₂(T) = 0.

## Definitions

- **Allowed p-path**: (v₀,...,v_p) with v_i→v_{i+1} for all i
- **DT 4-path**: (a,b,c,d) ∈ A₃ with a→b→c→d, a→c, b→d
- **Bad face of (a,b,c,d)**: a 2-face not in A₂
  - Face (a,c,d): bad iff c→a (not a→c)
  - Face (a,b,d): bad iff d→b (not b→d)
  - Faces (b,c,d) and (a,b,c) are always in A₂
- **Cancellation pair**: p₁ - p₂ where p₁, p₂ share a bad face f, so ∂(p₁-p₂) doesn't involve f

## Why DT paths are in Ω₃

A DT 4-path (a,b,c,d) has all 4 faces in A₂:
- (b,c,d): b→c ✓, c→d ✓ (from A₃)
- (a,b,c): a→b ✓, b→c ✓ (from A₃)
- (a,c,d): a→c ✓ (DT condition), c→d ✓
- (a,b,d): a→b ✓, b→d ✓ (DT condition)

Since A₁ is complete for tournaments (every pair has a directed edge),
all faces of faces are in A₁. So ∂(a,b,c,d) ∈ span(A₂) ⊆ Ω₁-compatible.
Therefore DT ⊆ Ω₃.

## Why cancellation pairs are in Ω₃

If p₁ = (a,b₁,c,d) and p₂ = (a,b₂,c,d) share bad face (a,c,d):
- ∂(p₁ - p₂) = [(b₁,c,d) - (b₂,c,d)] + [(a,b₁,d) - (a,b₂,d)] - [(a,b₁,c) - (a,b₂,c)]
- The -(a,c,d) terms cancel!
- Remaining terms may or may not be in A₂, but the non-A₂ terms
  correspond to OTHER bad faces, which can be handled by further cancellation.

More precisely: p₁ - p₂ ∈ Ω₃ iff all non-A₂ face terms cancel in ∂(p₁-p₂).
This is guaranteed when p₁, p₂ share exactly one bad face and their
other faces have the same A₂-membership pattern.

## Related Results

- **Surplus = ker(∂₃)**: dim(Ω₃) - dim(Z₂) = dim(ker ∂₃|Ω₃). Verified exactly at n=5.
  At surplus=0: ∂₃ is injective on Ω₃, β₃=0, and Ω₃ = DT paths only.
  At surplus=k: ker(∂₃) has dimension k, which becomes β₃ = k.

- **rk(∂₂) + rk(∂₃) = dim(Ω₂)**: Equivalent to β₂=0. Verified n=5 exhaustive.

- **Ω₂ dimension formula**: dim(Ω₂) = |TT| + Σ(mult_bad_face - 1). Verified n=5,6.

- **Relative homology**: H₂(T, T\v) = 0 for all T, v. Verified n=5,6 exhaustive (HYP-213).

## Missing for algebraic proof

The computational verification is complete through n=6 but an algebraic proof
that DT+cancellation boundaries span Z₂ is still needed. Two strategies:

1. **Direct construction**: Show every z ∈ Z₂ decomposes into DT+cancel boundaries
   by analyzing the structure of 2-cycles in terms of TT/NT path decomposition.

2. **Rank argument**: Show the combined boundary matrix from DT+cancel subspace
   to Z₂ has full rank, using dimension formulas and the structure of the
   junk matrix J₃.
